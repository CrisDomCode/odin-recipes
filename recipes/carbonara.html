Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

This classic Italian pasta dish is simple yet rich, combining crispy pancetta with a creamy egg and Parmesan sauce. Perfect for a quick yet indulgent meal, Spaghetti Carbonara is beloved for its minimal ingredients and maximum flavor.

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces spaghetti
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 4 ounces pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley (optional)

Steps

  1. Gather all your ingredients.
  2. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in boiling water, stirring occasionally until al dente, about 8 to 10 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  3. While the pasta is cooking, cook pancetta in a large skillet over medium heat until crispy, about 5 minutes. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together eggs and Parmesan cheese until well combined.
  5. Remove the skillet from heat and quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture into the hot pasta, tossing to combine. The residual heat will cook the eggs, creating a creamy sauce.
  6. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Sprinkle with chopped parsley if desired.
  7. Serve immediately, garnishing with additional Parmesan cheese if preferred.
